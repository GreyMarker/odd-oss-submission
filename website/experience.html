Restrict your data

Enable Restrictions (Screen Time)

Go to Settings > Screen Time.

Tap Turn On Screen Time and set it up (you may need to set a passcode).

Use Content & Privacy Restrictions to control which apps, features, and websites can be accessed.

Example: Restrict app downloads, explicit content, or location sharing.

Limit App Access to Data

Go to Settings > Privacy & Security.

Review and adjust permissions for apps (e.g., Camera, Microphone, Photos, Contacts).

Disable access for apps that don’t need certain permissions to function.

Restrict Background Data Usage

Go to Settings > Cellular (or Mobile Data).

Scroll through the list of apps and toggle off data access for apps you don’t want using cellular data.

For Wi-Fi restrictions, use Settings > Wi-Fi > (i) icon next to your network > Configure Proxy (if needed).

Use iCloud Private Relay (for Safari)

Go to Settings > [Your Name] > iCloud > Private Relay (requires iCloud+ subscription).

Enable Private Relay to encrypt and anonymize your Safari browsing data, restricting third-party tracking.

Set Up a VPN for Secure Data Flow

Install a trusted VPN app from the App Store.

Configure the VPN to encrypt all outgoing and incoming data, restricting unauthorized access.

Go to Settings > General > VPN & Device Management to manage your VPN settings.

Why we done it?

Learn more 􀆊

  • 7 Day waiting period.

    To access your data you have to request it. This can take up to 7 days.

  • Lengthy process to request.

    You have to sign up with a code and go to many different links that are not highlighted just to request your data.

  • Media attention.

    Different News outlets reported hackings or other issues when it comes to this topic over the years.

  • Different focus.

    In the outwards appearance apple takes this topic serious but digging deeper it seems they do not want to share every info with you.
